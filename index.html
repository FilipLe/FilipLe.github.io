NGUYEN'S BIO

About Nguyen Le.

Nguyen Le is currently one of the 20 Trustee Scholars at Boston University and is pursuing a B.A. in Computer Science at Boston University in Massachusetts, United States. He’s an aspiring mathematician and computer scientist, where he pursued many projects and activities to explore his passion.

One of the first projects he pursued was a lab equipment management system app to assist his school’s lab technician to manage school inventory. He also designed a medicine managing app to help his grandmother keep track of her medicine intake and inventory, created a Weather/SMS Alert program that sends personalised SMS alerts to inform users of weather conditions and suggest them suitable wear, and a Machine Learning KNN and Decision Tree models to predict housing prices.

In addition to that, he has also produced a series of Python tutorials, which are published to over 4000 people on Instagram. Nguyen also competed in international math contest such as ISMTF and IKMC, achieving up to 3rd place amongst roughly 300 people.

He is an avid learner, but also a community-oriented individual, where he has worked with NGOs such as NAE UNICEF and World Vision to act as a voice for vulnerable children around the world.

He has the vision of using technology in the pursuit of bringing people together and the vision of furthering his understanding of computers and how to utilize these powerful tools. Through technology, he hopes to unite people to complement each other and make our planet a better place.

Daily Dose of Weather (Weather SMS Alert system)
Project Navigator and Contributor, 2022
As part of the Boston Hacks competition, my team and I decided to create an alert system program that collects weather forecast information and sends users SMS text alerts reminding them suitable wear. We utilised the Open Weather API to retrieve weather forecast information within the upcoming 72 hours in our program and we made sure that the program refreshes every 6 hours so that the user is up-to-date with weather conditions and wears suitable clothes for that weather. We used SMTP Protocol Client API to create client session object that can be used to send users SMS text alerts of the weather and the clothing suggestion.
"Predicting Housing Prices using Publicly Available Datasets and Machine Learning"
Project Navigator and Contributor, 2022
Property appraisal is one of the most taxing task, especially in a rapidly changing property market nowadays, because it is a fairly imprecise discipline due to reliance on the unique features of the real estate property such as its location, size, property type, and economy growth. Achieving consistency in real estate appraisal is difficult; hence why, we wanted to build a solution that is capable of predicting the house prices better and with greater consistency and precision than human appraisers.

To explore this issue, we built Machine Learning models such as K-Nearest Neighbors and Decision Trees Classifier Models and used our classifier models with DataFrames on the given metrics to manipulate and evaluate our data to determine an approximate predicted valuation of a property.‍

Lab Equipment Management System
Creator, 2020 - 2021
